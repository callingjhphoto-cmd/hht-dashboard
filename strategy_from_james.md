# HHT — Strategy Notes From James
**Recorded:** 16 March 2026

## Joe's Business
- **Revenue:** ~£1.75M/year (events side)
- **Staff costs:** ~£500K/year (25% overhead — actually good for hospitality)
- **Profit margin:** ~25% if managed well
- **Staff roles:** People sending emails, organising events, CRM work — £25-40K/year each
- **Current SaaS:** CRM platform (unknown which), Dropbox (hates paying monthly), others TBC

## Joe's Mindset
- NOT focused on cost-cutting — focused on REVENUE GENERATION
- Wants more leads, more events, more growth
- Considering hiring a salesperson at £50-60K/year to generate leads
- Classic founder dilemma: good income, but wants to scale and eventually retire
- Hates monthly SaaS subscriptions (e.g. Dropbox)
- Wants: one dashboard, one platform, stored locally or on his own server

## What James Proposed
- Dashboard that replaces all his SaaS products
- Integrates: CRM, recipe management, data scraping for leads, organisational tasks
- AI automation of tasks currently done by £25-40K/year staff
- First priority: LEAD GENERATION through data scraping

## HHT Business Model — Two Arms
### 1. Events (the scalable arm) — PRIORITY
- Cocktail parties, weddings, corporate events, birthday parties
- Mobile bartending/cocktail services at various locations
- Sends teams out — scalable with more leads
- This is where the growth is
- Revenue generation = more leads = more events

### 2. Brand Consultancy (smaller arm)
- Brand consulting for drinks companies
- Drinks styling for photo shoots
- Smaller, less scalable industry
- Not the growth driver

## What Joe Needs (in priority order)
1. **Lead generation scrapers** — army of bots finding potential clients
   - Wedding venues, corporate event planners, birthday party organisers
   - Hotels, restaurants wanting cocktail services
   - Brands wanting activations
   - Geographic targeting (where does HHT operate?)
2. **Lead qualification** — which leads are worth pursuing
3. **Automated outreach** — templated but personalised emails/messages
4. **CRM replacement** — integrated into the dashboard, no monthly fee
5. **Event management** — scheduling, staffing, logistics
6. **Recipe management** — cocktail recipes, stock lists, event packs
7. **Financial overview** — revenue tracking, cost per event, margin analysis

## The Scraping Strategy
- Very specific industry: mobile cocktail services / event bartending
- Targets:
  - Wedding planners and venues (biggest market)
  - Corporate event managers
  - Hotels without in-house cocktail capability
  - PR/marketing agencies running brand activations
  - Private party organisers
  - Festival/music event organisers
- Data sources:
  - Google Maps (venues, hotels, event spaces)
  - Wedding directories (Hitched, Bridebook, WeddingWire)
  - LinkedIn (event managers, corporate PA's)
  - Instagram (venues posting events)
  - Companies House (new hospitality businesses)
  - Event industry publications
  - Competitor monitoring (The Cocktail Service went into administration — their clients need a new provider)

## Revenue Potential for James
- If we save Joe the £50-60K salesperson hire by building scrapers that generate equivalent leads, that's the value proposition
- Monthly retainer: £1-3K/month for lead generation + dashboard maintenance
- Or: percentage of revenue generated from leads (performance-based)
- Or: one-off build fee + smaller monthly maintenance

## Key Insight
Joe doesn't want to hear about saving money. He wants to hear about MAKING money. The pitch isn't "we'll replace your staff with AI." It's "we'll find you 10x more leads than a £60K salesperson could, for a fraction of the cost."
