## MARKET OVERVIEW

### Industry Size
- UK events market: ~$86bn (2025), projected ~$99bn by 2035 (CAGR ~1%)
- UK event catering segment: ~GBP 1.4bn (2025), growing at CAGR 3.9% to 2030
- UK pubs & bars market: ~GBP 24.9bn (2026)
- Corporate event spending UK: projected GBP 2bn (2025)
- Mobile bar hire is a sub-segment — no standalone market sizing exists, but estimated hundreds of millions based on number of operators and event volumes

### Seasonal Patterns
- **Peak season:** May-September (wedding season, summer corporates, festivals)
- **Shoulder season:** March-April, September-November (25-27% of weddings fall here)
- **Off-peak:** December (Christmas parties spike), January-February (quietest)
- **Key insight:** More couples choosing off-peak/midweek dates to save costs — opportunity for operators to fill calendar year-round

### Average Pricing (2025 data)
| Service Type | Price Range |
|---|---|
| Dry hire (bar + staff only) | GBP 300-800 |
| All-inclusive mobile bar (standard) | GBP 500-2,000 |
| Per head (standard drinks) | GBP 25-50 |
| Per head (premium cocktails) | GBP 60+ |
| Individual cocktail price | GBP 8-12 per drink |
| Bartender hire (3-4 hrs) | GBP 250-400 |
| Flair bartender upgrade | +GBP 150 |
| Large-scale events | Up to GBP 20,000 |
| Cash bar minimum spend | GBP 500-3,000+ |

### Key Industry Bodies & Directories
- **HAE/EHA** (Hire Association Europe / Event Hire Association) — 3,500+ member businesses
- **MIA** (Meetings Industry Association) — keeper of AIM quality standard
- **BVEP** (Business Visits & Events Partnership) — umbrella advocacy body
- **EMA** (Events Management Association) — monthly events, government representation
- **EVCOM** — 600+ members across live events, film, digital
- **HBAA** (Hospitality Business Association) — quality assurance mark
- **Key directories:** Add to Event, Poptop, Togather, Alive Network, Bridebook, DesignMyNight

## COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS SUMMARY

### Market Structure
The UK mobile cocktail bar market is **highly fragmented** with hundreds of small operators. Most are:
- 1-5 person operations (founder + freelance bartenders)
- Wedding-focused with corporate as secondary
- London-concentrated but many regional players
- Competing primarily on price and bar aesthetics

### HHT's Competitive Advantages
1. **Credibility gap:** Joe Stokoe's CV (Milk & Honey, Trailer Happiness, Quo Vadis) is unmatched in this space. Most competitors are run by event planners, not world-class bartenders.
2. **Consultancy capability:** HHT's drinks development kitchen in Camden is unique. No other mobile bar company has R&D infrastructure.
3. **Brand-level work:** Most competitors do weddings and parties. HHT works with spirit brands and creative agencies — a completely different tier.
4. **The Cocktail Service vacuum:** Their administration creates an immediate opportunity to capture displaced clients, especially brand activation and drinks agency accounts.

### Key Threats
1. **Twist Events** — Most direct competitor. 3,000+ events, 50 Best Bars connection, celebrity/brand clients. They are the company to watch.
2. **Quirky Group** — Strong on brand activations and promotional campaigns. Different model (production agency) but competes for same brand budgets.
3. **Mix & Twist** — Nationwide coverage, premium automotive brand clients.
4. **Price competition:** Most operators charge GBP 25-50/head. HHT's consultancy-grade positioning needs to justify premium pricing.

### Gaps in the Market (Opportunities for HHT)
1. **Drinks consultancy for venues** — Very few mobile bar companies offer menu development/consultancy to bars and restaurants
2. **Spirit brand activations post-Cocktail Service** — Immediate land grab
3. **Festival bar operations** — Growing segment, HHT already has festival credentials
4. **Content/media** — Joe's expertise could fuel thought leadership that competitors lack
5. **International** — Limited competitors operate outside UK. HHT's international network is an advantage.
