## TOP 5 COMPETITORS YOU'LL MEET IN THE FIELD

---

### 1. SWEET & CHILLI
**Their position:** Fun party brand, mobile bar logistics. Mid-range (£25-40/head).
**Their weakness:** Zero consultancy. Zero R&D. Same templated cocktail list every event. No Camden kitchen, no bespoke development, no brand relationships. Works for hen dos, not premium corporate.
**You beat them by:** "We develop drinks — they deploy drinks. We'll create a cocktail that tells your brand story. They'll give you a mojito from a laminated card."
**Objection handler:** *"Sweet & Chilli are cheaper."*
> "They are. You're also paying for someone who's never created a cocktail — they rent out bars and hire staff from an agency. We charge more because we design and develop the whole experience in-house. If your event needs to be memorable, not just functional, that matters."

---

### 2. CREATE COCKTAILS
**Their position:** Premium-ish cocktail catering, masterclasses. Mid-to-high (£35-55/head).
**Their weakness:** Founders come from events, not the bar industry. No industry credentials. No spirit brand relationships. Maxes out at 150-200 guests. Can't co-fund events through brand partnerships.
**You beat them by:** Joe's 25-year CV. Milk & Honey. Trailer Happiness. Real spirit brand access. "I know the brand managers at Bombay Sapphire and Belvedere personally. That opens doors to partnership funding that reduces your costs."
**Objection handler:** *"We've used Create Cocktails before and they were fine."*
> "Good — you already know the value of proper cocktail service. The difference is we can bring brand partnerships to the table. I work directly with spirit brands, which means I can often secure co-funding or product supply that offsets your costs. And our menus are developed in our Camden kitchen, not pulled from a standard list."

### 3. COTSWOLD BAR COMPANY
**Their position:** Local Cotswolds regional player, rustic aesthetic, weddings. Low-to-mid (£20-35/head).
**Their weakness:** They're selling furniture — the look of the bar. Drinks quality is secondary. Casual agency bartenders, no training programme. No brand work, no corporate pipeline. Growth ceiling = local wedding market.
**You beat them by:** Trained HHT bartenders (not temps from a staffing app). Bespoke cocktail menus developed in Camden. London credibility brought to Cotswolds venues.
**Objection handler:** *"The Cotswold Bar Company is local and knows the area."*
> "They do — and they're great for a certain type of event. We're now expanding into the Cotswolds precisely because clients asked us to. We bring the same bartenders and the same development kitchen that serves our London brand clients. Every cocktail is designed for your event, not pulled from a regional company's standard list. We charge more, but so does every venue in the Cotswolds that takes quality seriously."

### 4. OXFORD BAR COMPANY
**Their position:** Local Oxford/Oxfordshire specialist, mid-range (£30-45/head). Most likely to have picked up Cocktail Service orphans.
**Their weakness:** They inherited Cocktail Service clients but lack the consultancy depth. They offer "pick 3 from our list of 20" — not bespoke creation. No London corporate contacts. No brand activation experience. No development kitchen.
**You beat them by:** Fill the exact gap The Cocktail Service left. Former Cocktail Service clients expected consultancy-grade drinks development — Oxford Bar Company can't match that. HHT can.
**Objection handler:** *"We used The Cocktail Service before they closed. Oxford Bar Company took over."*
> "That makes sense as an immediate fix. What The Cocktail Service was known for — the genuine drinks consultancy, the bespoke menus, the R&D — Oxford Bar Company can't replicate that. We can. Same consultancy-grade capability, now serving Oxfordshire. If what you valued was the expertise, we're the natural replacement."

### 5. OSCARS BAR HIRE
**Their position:** Volume operator, budget end (£20-35/head). Fun/accessible brand.
**Their weakness:** Volume model = inconsistent staff quality. No bespoke anything. Brand screams budget. Vulnerable to bad reviews. Zero creative or intellectual property.
**You beat them by:** Consistency, quality control, reputation protection. "What happens when the bartender they send doesn't meet your guests' expectations?"
**Objection handler:** *"Oscars are half the price."*
> "They're a different product. If your venue has a reputation to protect, you need a bar partner that matches your standard. Inconsistency is Oscars' biggest risk — high-volume operators use large pools of casual staff and quality varies. We run a smaller operation with trained bartenders we know personally. One bad event is far more expensive than the saving on the bar."
