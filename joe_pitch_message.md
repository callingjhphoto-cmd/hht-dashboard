## NOTES FOR JAMES

**Word count:** 207
**Tone:** Casual but substantive — shows the work without overselling

**Key hooks in this message:**
1. **392 venues + 58 competitors** — shows scale and effort already invested
2. **The Cocktail Service collapse** — Joe will know them, this is the attention-grabber
3. **£20K+ in orphaned bookings** — specific revenue figure makes it tangible
4. **308 qualified targets** — not just a list, it's been filtered and scored
5. **Free for 30 days, then £200/mo** — removes all risk, trivial ongoing cost
6. **Dashboard link** — he can see it immediately

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't lead with the price — the value comes first
- Don't apologise for the length — this is a pitch, not a text
- Don't say "let me know" — ask for a specific action (15 mins this week)
- Don't mention the technical stack — Joe doesn't care about React or Vite

**If Joe pushes back on £200/mo:**
- "It's less than one wedding cocktail booking"
- "Apollo.io charges $99/mo and you'd have to do all the work yourself"
- "I'm literally delivering qualified leads with phone numbers and emails — that alone saves you hours per week"

**If Joe asks "what's in it for you?":**
- Be honest: "I want a retainer client for ongoing work. If this works for HHT, it's a model I can replicate for other service businesses."

**If Joe says "let me think about it":**
- Follow up in 3 days: "Hey Joe, did you get a chance to look at the dashboard? Happy to jump on a quick screenshare if that's easier."
